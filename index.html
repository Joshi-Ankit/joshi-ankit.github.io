About

My aim is to enable precise forecasting of thermal injury, so that it is predicted and prevented rather than treated after it occurs. More people are exposed to extreme environments today than at any point in recorded history, yet thermal risk is still assessed using population averages and fixed environmental thresholds that do not account for individual physiological variability. To close this gap, I work to establish individualized thermoregulation modeling as a discipline that predicts risk at the level of the person rather than the population.

My modelling work covers the full spectrum of anatomical detail, from high-fidelity 3D virtual humans that resolve heat transfer at the organ level to reduced-order surrogates efficient enough for real-time prediction, and across the human lifespan from the young and healthy to the elderly and even the fetus. By pairing this range with real-time sensing and early warning, I am building toward a future in which every individual, in any thermal environment, can know their own safe limits before those safe limits are crossed.

  • aIndividualized thermoregulation modeling — extending bioheat models beyond the standard adult to capture physiologically distinct groups, including young healthy adults, the elderly, and the fetus, and using these models to evaluate heat mitigation strategies such as fan cooling, water dousing, and cold-water immersion.

Research Areas

Individualized Thermoregulation Modeling

Building physiological models that account for individual characteristics — extending beyond the standard adult to the elderly, fetus, and diverse occupational populations. Models range from high-fidelity 3D finite-element representations to fast reduced-order surrogates for real-time use.

Clothing & PPE Heat Transfer

Analytical and computational characterization of heat and mass transport through textile and protective equipment — wet conduction, evaporative cooling, garment microclimate — validated with thermal manikins to optimize firefighter gloves, exosuits, and occupational workwear.

Real-Time Sensing & Early Warning

Mobile instrumentation platforms that combine environmental sensing with physiological modeling to produce person-specific, real-time heat strain estimates — translating computational predictions into deployable tools for occupational health and clinical decision support.

Thermal Manikin Experimentation

Experimental characterization of convective, radiative, and evaporative heat exchange under realistic conditions — outdoor solar radiation, turbulence, walking motion, and body morphology — generating validated coefficients for computational models.

Heat Mitigation & Intervention

Quantitative evaluation of cooling interventions across physiologically distinct populations. Research directly informs evidence-based guidelines for heat casualty prevention in military, industrial, and clinical settings.

Vulnerable Population Modeling

Extending thermoregulation modeling to populations with distinct physiology — elderly individuals, fetal thermal environments — to characterize elevated heat risk and support targeted public health policy for extreme heat events.
