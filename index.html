My aim is to enable precise forecasting of thermal injury, so that it is predicted and prevented rather than treated after it occurs. More people are exposed to extreme environments today than at any point in recorded history, yet thermal risk is still assessed using population averages and fixed environmental thresholds that do not account for individual physiological variability. To close this gap, I work to establish individualized thermoregulation modeling as a discipline that predicts risk at the level of the person rather than the population.

My models span the full landscape of the field — from high-fidelity 3D virtual humans that resolve heat transfer at the organ level to reduced-order models efficient enough for real-time prediction — and across the human lifespan from the young and healthy to the elderly and even the fetus. By pairing this range with real-time sensing and early warning, I am building toward a future in which every individual, in any environment, can know their own thermal limits before those limits are crossed.

Research Areas

Individualized Thermoregulation Modeling

Building biophysical models of human thermoregulation that account for individual physiology — extending beyond the standard adult to the elderly, the fetus, and distinct occupational populations. Models span high-fidelity 3D finite-element representations to fast reduced-order surrogates suitable for real-time deployment.

Clothing & PPE Heat Transfer

Analytical and computational characterization of heat and mass transport through textile and protective equipment layers — including wet conduction, evaporative cooling, and garment microclimate — validated against thermal manikin experiments and used to optimize design for firefighter gloves, exosuits, and occupational workwear.

Real-Time Sensing & Early Warning

Development of mobile instrumentation platforms that combine environmental sensing with physiological modeling to produce person-specific, real-time heat strain estimates. The goal is translating computational predictions into deployable tools for occupational health monitoring and clinical decision support.

Thermal Manikin Experimentation

Experimental characterization of convective, radiative, and evaporative heat exchange from the full human body under realistic environmental conditions — including outdoor solar radiation, turbulence, walking motion, and body morphology effects — generating validated coefficients for computational models.

Heat Mitigation & Intervention

Quantitative evaluation of cooling interventions — fan-only, water dousing, and cold-water immersion — across physiologically distinct populations using thermoregulation models. Research directly informs evidence-based guidelines for heat casualty prevention in military, industrial, and clinical settings.

Vulnerable Population Modeling

Extending thermoregulation modeling to populations with distinct physiological characteristics — elderly individuals with impaired thermoeffector responses, pregnant women and fetal thermal environments — to characterize elevated heat risk and support targeted public health policies for extreme heat events.
